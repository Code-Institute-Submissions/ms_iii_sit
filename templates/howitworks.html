How to use the MBPM Service Issue Tracker

Your place of residence is your castle, your sanctuary, your place of peace and happiness, the place you look forward to going back to at the end of a long day of work. Unfortunately, as we all know, things can break and unforseen events can happen that disrupt that peace and happiness and can make your residence not feel so much like a sanctuary.

MBPM is here to ensure that whatever goes wrong gets resolved as quickly as possible, reinstating your sanctuary as little disruption as possible. To help us do that, we have created this web site that allows you to report an issue with us. You can track its resolution progress and utimately what the resolution whas. We have worked really hard at MBPM to make the service issue tracker (SIT) as intuitive as possible. However, if you need a little more guidance, please read on.

There has been no successful software product that has had only a version 1! Our best marketing is you, our customer. To that end, on the main Unicorn Accelerator dashboard page you have the ability to request new features.

Below is an explanation how both services work. Click on the links to expand the section that goes into detail on how to use each service.

Issues Reporting and Tracking Service

1
Register with Unicorn Acclerator

You do not need an account to view the issues reported However, to upvote an issue, or enter a new issue, you must be have an UA account and be logged in.

2
Upvote an issue

Once logged in, if you see an issue that has been reported that looks familiar and want to upvote it, click on the "Show More" link to expand the text to see more details about the issue. Click on theicon to upvote the issue.

3
Enter an issue

If you have run into an issue with Unicorn Accelerator and are logged in, you may enter a new issue by clicking on the "Report Issue" button on the "UA Issues" tab on the dashboard on the main page of our site. Note: that button remains disabled until you are logged in.

Issues have 3 statuses:

  • Reported
  • Ongoing
  • Closed

When you first create an issue, it has a status of "Reported". Once someone at UA has seen the issue and acknowledged it, it then is promoted to "Ongoing" meaning we are working on it. And finally, once we have fixed the issue, the status gets promoted to "Closed".

On the UA Issues page you have the ability to filter the issues that display. You have are able to filter on the statuses mentioned previously as well as "Show All" and the viewing only those issues that you entered.

Features Request Service

1
Register with Unicorn Acclerator

You do not need an account to view features that have been requested. However, to request a feature, you must be have an UA account and be logged in.

2
Request a feature

Once logged in, you can request a feature to be added to Unicorn Accelerator by clicking on the "Request Feature" button on the "UA Features" tab on the dashboard on the main page of our site. Note: that button remains disabled until you are logged in.

3
Feature Acceptance

There are 5 statuses that a feature can be in:

  1. Requested
  2. Accepted - Waiting Payment
  3. Accepted - Working
  4. Denied
  5. Finished

On the UA Requested Features page you have the ability to filter the features that display. You are able to filter on the statuses mentioned previously as well as "Show All" and the to view only those features that you have requested.

Requested is the status of the request when you first make the feature request. When you request a feature you give it a title and then a detailed description. If we have more questions about the feature, we will contact you via email and possibly set up a meeting if needed.

What is key about requesting a feature is the bidding process. Every feature that is requested must have a bid (in GBP) associated with it. This bid is entered at time of entering the details about the feature. The bid can be between £10.00 and £5000.00. In most cases UA will prioritise feature requests based on amount bid. Therefore, the higher the bid, the more likely it will get accepted.

Once we have accepted the feature, the feature status will be listed in the dashboard as Accepted with this  icon next to it. At this point, you can click on that icon where it will take you to a page to enter your credit card details.

Once the payment has been processed successfully, the status of the request will show as Accepted with this  icon next to it.

Once the feature has been completed it will be promoted to a status of Finished and automatically pushed out. We will notify you via email upon completion.

Requests will be Denied if the bids are too low and/or we have a many other requests at much higher bids.
