How to use the MBPM Service Issue Tracker

Your place of residence is your castle, your sanctuary, your place of peace and happiness, the place you look forward to going back to at the end of a long day of work. Unfortunately, as we all know, things can break and unforseen events can happen that disrupt that peace and happiness and can make your residence not feel so much like your sanctuary.

MBPM is here to ensure that whatever goes wrong is resolved as quickly as possible, putting your sanctuary back to the way it was with as little disruption as possible. To help us do that, we have created this web site that allows you to report an issue with us. You can track its progress towards resolution as well as how the issue was resolved. We have worked really hard at MBPM to make the service issue tracker (SIT) as intuitive as possible and available on all device types. However, if you need a little more guidance, please read on.

Issues Reporting and Tracking Service

1
Register with MBPM

In order to view or create issues, you must have a MBPM account. Click the login button or menu item on the MBPM landing page it will take you to the login page that allows you to log in or create an account.

2
Log an issue

If you have run into an issue and are logged in, you may enter a new issue by clicking on the "Add Issues" button below the list of existing issues. Note: you cannot get to the issues page unless you are logged in.

When entering a issue, you will be require to choose a category for the issue. Not all issues are bad! Below are the categories of issues:

  • Comment - For making general comments about your property or our service.
  • Complaint - This could range from something we may be doing wrong to building tenants putting things in communal areas.
  • Question - A general inquiry, for example a question about your service charge.
  • Repair - Something on your property needs repairing.
  • Suggestion - For example, something we could do better or something that could improve the property.

Give the issue a title/subject and provide a detailed description. You also have the ability to categorise it as an urgent issue. We ask that you please be discretionary with this setting. On a laptop or desktop device, once you save the issue you will see it as the very first item on the issues page with a either a    if the issue is urgent or a    for non-urgent issues. This acts as a link enabling you to edit the issue. You may also edit the issue by clicking on the issue id. On smaller devices, you will see a button labeled "Edit Issue". It will be red if the issue is urgent.

3
Viewing and Filtering Issues

The main issues dashboard gives you an overview of each issue. As previously stated, you may only edit issues that you have created. Issues that were not created by you will have this    icon next to them. If it is urgent, the icon will be red. You can view the details of an issue anyone has created by clicking on this    widget that is left of the listed issue. Below the issue a section expands showing more detail about the issue, including its current status.

Issues have 3 statuses:

  • Not Viewed - A newly created issue that has yet to be viewed by MBPM
  • Viewed - This has been viewed by MBPM and we have been in touch with you discussing next steps
  • Resolved - The issue has been resolved

You may filter what issues display on the dashboard. To filter by category and/or issue status, click on this    in the upper right hand of the dashboard panel. When clicked, a dialog box appears allowing you to toggle on and off which categories with what statuses you would like to see. Just click on the green circle and it will become red, indicating you do not want to see that status or category. Click it again, it will turn green indicating you do want it included in your filter criteria. You must have at least one category and one status selected. Click the Filter button and you will be taken back to the dashboard showing only those categories and statuses included in the filter. When you have a filter applied, the filter icon changes to this — Filter Applied   — on the dashboard.

You also have the ability to search issues. Entering in search criteria will search both the issue subject/title and the issue description fields for the text you enter. To perform a search, enter the text you would like to search for in the search box at the top of the issues panel. You can enter up to 60 characters. You may search for multiple terms by separating the text with a ",". When you do that, it creates an "or" statement. This means that if you enter something like "rubbish, sink, floor" that all issues is which the issue subject or description contains "rubbish" or "sink" or "floor" will be returned from the database and display in the panel. If you do not separate terms with commas, then the entire text will be searched; for example, "rubbish sink floor" will look for all issues that contain that exact text in aforementioned fields. Click this    icon to perform the search. Click this    icon to clear the search. Note: if you currently have filter applied, performing a search clears the filter.

MBPM Administrators

1
Issues Statuses

As an MBPM administrator you have the ability to view and edit all issues. It is also your responsibility to change the status of issues as they progress — first to Viewed once you have contacted the tenant regarding their issue and then to resolved once the issue is resolved. See the Log Issue and the Viewing and Filtering sections for more information. You may also mark an issue as resolved even though it is in the "Not Viewed" state. See more below.

Although you can edit an issue, the only way an issue status can be changed is in the expandable detail section of the issue on the Issues dashboard. Click on this    widget that is left of the listed issue. Below the issue a section expands showing more detail about the issue, including its current status. In the row displaying the status, you will see this    icon if the tenant has not been contacted by MBPM. Once you contact the tenant, click this icon to change the status to "Viewed". The status changes automatically in the dashboard and that icon will no longer be there.

For all issues that have NOT been resolved, you will see this    icon. Click this to mark an issue as resolved. When you click that, a dialog box appears requiring you to enter details as to how the issue was resolved. The resolution will appear in the details of the issue on the issues dashboard. Once the issue has been resolved, the owner/creator of the issue will no longer be able to edit it and the icon next to the issue on the dashboard will be the    icon. At that point, clicking on it will do nothing more than display the detail.

2
Categories

As mentioned above, there are standard catgories out of the box. However, as an administrator you have the ability to create new categories as well as edit any category. However, you are not able to delete a category. You can mark a category as inactive. When doing so, issues with that category no longer display in the issues dashboard nor does the category show up in the filter dialog box.

To get to the Categories dashboard, click on the "Categories" menu option or "Categories" button on the landing page. These are only available to administrators. An administrator will have the    icon next to the "Profile" menu item, whilst regular users will have the    icon next to it.

To add a category, simple click the "Add Categories" button at the bottom of the list on the Categories dashboard. To edit a category, click the    next to the category on the dashboard.
